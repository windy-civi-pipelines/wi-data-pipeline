Referred to Committee on Children and Families.AB237,1,31An Act to amend 48.195 (1m) (a) (intro.), 48.355 (2d) (b) 5. and 48.415 (1m) of 2the statutes; relating to: the age at which an infant is covered under the safe 3haven law.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauUnder current law, commonly referred to as the “safe haven law,” a child whom a law enforcement officer, emergency medical technician, or hospital staff member reasonably believes to be 72 hours old or younger (newborn infant) may be taken into custody under circumstances in which a parent of the newborn infant relinquishes custody of the newborn infant to the law enforcement officer, emergency medical technician, or hospital staff member and does not express an intent to return for the newborn infant or in which a parent of the newborn infant leaves the child in a newborn infant safety device installed in a supporting wall of a hospital, fire station, or law enforcement agency. Under current law, a parent who relinquishes custody of a child under the safe haven law and any person who assists the parent in that relinquishment are immune from any civil or criminal liability for any good faith act or omission in connection with the relinquishment. This bill changes the maximum age at which a newborn infant falls under the safe haven law from 72 hours old to 30 days old.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB237,11Section 1. 48.195 (1m) (a) (intro.) of the statutes is amended to read:AB237,2,7248.195 (1m) (a) (intro.)
