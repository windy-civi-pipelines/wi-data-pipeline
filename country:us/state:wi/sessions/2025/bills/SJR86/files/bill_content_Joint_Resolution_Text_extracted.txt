Referred to Committee on Senate Organization.SJR86,1,11Relating to: recognizing the United States Navy’s 250th birthday.SJR86,1,42Whereas, the U.S. Navy traces its origins to a vote taken by the Continental 3Congress on October 13, 1775, to outfit two sailing vessels for the interception of 4ships that were transporting munitions to the British Army in America; andSJR86,1,55Whereas, October 13, 2025, marks 250 years since this historic date; andSJR86,1,76Whereas, in our celebration of the U.S. Navy’s 250th birthday, we reflect on its 7legacy of defending our country’s freedoms, values, and interests; andSJR86,1,108Whereas, from the Revolutionary War to the present day, our sailors have 9courageously defended the United States, its citizens, and its Constitution with the 10utmost dedication and resolve; andSJR86,1,1211Whereas, over 330,000 active duty sailors serve our country in accordance 12with the U.S. Navy’s core values of honor, courage, and commitment; andSJR86,2,213Whereas, Wisconsin has played a vital role in supporting the U.S. Navy 1through its industrial, technological, and personnel contributions, serving as a key 2partner in preserving America’s maritime strength; andSJR86,2,63Whereas, Wisconsin’s rich shipbuilding history includes the construction of a 4breadth of ships for the U.S. Navy, ranging from submarine chasers built by the 5Leathem D. Smith Shipbuilding Company to tank landing ships and landing craft 6utilities built by the Christy Corporation; andSJR86,2,97Whereas, Wisconsin continues to aid the U.S. Navy in its mission to protect 8the nation, notably with the production of the Freedom-class littoral combat ship 9and the Constellation-class guided-missile frigate; andSJR86,2,1110Whereas, at least 30 past and current U.S. Navy ships have been named after 11the state of Wisconsin and its cities, counties, landmarks, and people; andSJR86,2,1512Whereas, two battleships have proudly borne the name USS Wisconsin: BB-9, 13the Illinois-class battleship that toured the world as part of the famed Great White 14Fleet, and BB-64, the Iowa-class battleship that earned a total of six battle stars for 15her service in World War II and the Korean War; andSJR86,2,1816Whereas, the U.S. Navy and its sailors, reserves, and civilian personnel will 17continue to serve their country with relentless vigor and bravery, no matter the 18challenge before them; andSJR86,2,2119Whereas, Wisconsin deeply appreciates the immense sacrifices made by the 20U.S. Navy’s past and present sailors in its 250 years of proud service; now, 21therefore, be itSJR86,3,222Resolved by the senate, the assembly concurring, That the Wisconsin 1Legislature celebrates the U.S. Navy’s 250th birthday on October 13, 2025, and 2recognizes its rich history and legacy of service.SJR86,3,33(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/sjr86 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/sjr86 proposaltext/2025/REG/SJR86 proposaltext/2025/REG/SJR86 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»SJR86: Joint Resolution Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
