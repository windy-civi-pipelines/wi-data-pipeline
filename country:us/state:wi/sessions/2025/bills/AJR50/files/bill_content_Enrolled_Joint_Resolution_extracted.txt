andWhereas, as we celebrate the 250th birthday of the U.S. Army, we reflect on its rich history and legacy of service, sacrifice, and dedication; andWhereas, from the Revolutionary War to present-day missions, our soldiers have consistently demonstrated their resolve to defend freedom at home and abroad; andWhereas, for 250 years, U.S. Army soldiers, families, and Army civilians have supported our nation, bearing true faith and allegiance to the country, the U.S. Constitution, and the Army; andWhereas, America’s Army is the largest of the armed services, consisting of more than 1.2 million people across the globe who defend our country every day; andWhereas, today's Army stands ready to meet any challenge and will continue to adapt and modernize to remain the most lethal ground force in the world; andWhereas, more than 10,000 Army soldiers and civilians are currently stationed or working in Wisconsin, and more than 100,000 Army veterans call Wisconsin home; andWhereas, since it was established in 1909, U.S. Army Garrison Fort McCoy has played a crucial role in our nation’s defense and growth as a nation, both as a mobilization and training site for military personnel and as a select location for housing displaced persons; andWhereas, Fort McCoy is the only U.S. Army installation in Wisconsin, has a local and regional economic impact of nearly $1.4 billion, and remains one of Monroe County’s largest employers; andWhereas, each Army soldier serves according to the Army Values of loyalty, duty, respect, selfless service, honor, integrity, and personal courage; and Whereas, Army soldiers and civilians will continue to maintain the trust and confidence of the American people while adding to the proud legacy of the Army; andWhereas, Wisconsin honors and appreciates its Army soldiers, civilians, and veterans and their legacy of brave service to our state and nation as a whole; now, therefore, be itResolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the Wisconsin Legislature celebrates the U.S. Army’s 250th birthday on June 14, 2025, and recognizes its rich history and legacy of service, sacrifice, and dedication.
