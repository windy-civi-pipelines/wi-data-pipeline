Referred to Committee on Rules.AJR97,1,11Relating to: proclaiming October 2025 as Careers in Construction Month.AJR97,1,42Whereas, Careers in Construction Month is designated annually to increase 3public awareness and appreciation of construction craft professionals and the entire 4construction workforce; andAJR97,1,75Whereas, the construction industry is one of Wisconsin’s largest industries, 6with nearly 6 percent of the Wisconsin workforce being employed in construction; 7andAJR97,1,118Whereas, construction is a true opportunity career, allowing anyone to start as 9a general laborer, become an apprentice, develop a specialization, gain licensing and 10credentials, advance into a supervisory role, and eventually become their own boss; 11andAJR97,1,1312Whereas, construction is a lifelong career where you can raise a family in a 13career that cannot be outsourced; andAJR97,2,21Whereas, Wisconsin’s world class apprenticeship program allows individuals 2to earn when they learn without accumulating overwhelming student debt; andAJR97,2,53Whereas, our fellow Wisconsinites work every day in construction, building 4the houses we live in, the schools we learn in, the hospitals we are healed in, and the 5houses of worship we pray in; andAJR97,2,76Whereas, in Wisconsin, the construction industry needs nearly 500,000 new 7craft professionals in 2026, according to Associated Builders and Contractors; andAJR97,2,98Whereas, we are pleased to honor construction craft professionals and the 9critical role they play in the development and growth of our state; andAJR97,2,1310Whereas, during this month of October, employers, associations, and schools 11are encouraged to conduct job fairs, panel discussions, and local community events 12to inform students of the vast employment opportunities in construction that lead 13to rewarding, long-term careers; andAJR97,2,1814Whereas, this month, the state of Wisconsin joins dedicated individuals and 15organizations across our state in honoring our state’s construction craft 16professionals for their hard work and dedication and recognizing the critical role 17they play in the continued development and maintenance of our state;
