Referred to Committee on Health, Aging and Long-Term Care.AB128,1,21An Act to create 255.075 of the statutes; relating to: requiring first 2responders to be trained to administer epinephrine delivery systems.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill requires first responders to be trained in how to recognize signs and symptoms of severe allergic reactions, standards and procedures for the storage and administration of an epinephrine delivery system, and emergency follow-up procedures after an epinephrine delivery system is administered and requires first responders to have an epinephrine delivery system available to the first responder for use at all times while on duty. First responders include conservation wardens, correctional officers, emergency medical responders, emergency medical services practitioners, firefighters, and law enforcement officers. The Department of Health Services is required to identify organizations that conduct trainings that cover all the subjects that first responders are required to be trained in under the bill. Finally, the bill allows DHS to distribute epinephrine delivery systems to first responders who are trained in all subjects described under the bill or to employers of first responders who are trained in all subjects described under the bill to be used by those first responders.For further information see the state and local fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB128,11Section 1. 255.075 of the statutes is created to read:AB128,2,32255.075 Use of epinephrine delivery systems by first responders. (1) 3In this section:AB128,2,54(a) “Administer” means to directly apply an epinephrine delivery system to 5the body of an individual.AB128,2,66(b) “Epinephrine delivery system” has the meaning given in s. 255.07 (1) (cm).AB128,2,77(c) “First responder” means any of the following:AB128,2,881. Conservation warden under s. 23.10.AB128,2,992. Correctional officer, as defined in s. 102.475 (8) (a).AB128,2,10103. Emergency medical responder, as defined in s. 256.01 (4p).AB128,2,11114. Emergency medical services practitioner, as defined in s. 256.01 (5).AB128,2,12125. Firefighter, as defined in s. 36.27 (3m) (a) 1m.AB128,2,13136. Law enforcement officer, as defined in s. 165.85 (2) (c).AB128,2,1714(2) The department shall identify organizations that conduct trainings, either 15online or in person, that cover all of the subjects required under sub. (3). The 16department shall compile a list of all organizations identified under this subsection, 17post the list on the department’s website, and update the list as necessary.AB128,2,1818(3) Each first responder shall be trained in all of the following:AB128,2,2019(a) How to recognize signs and symptoms of severe allergic reactions, 20including anaphylaxis.AB128,2,2221(b) Standards and procedures for the storage and administration of an 22epinephrine delivery system.AB128,3,21(c) Emergency follow-up procedures after an epinephrine delivery system is 2administered.AB128,3,43(4) Each first responder shall have an epinephrine delivery system available 4to the first responder for use at all times while on duty.AB128,3,85(5) From the appropriation under s. 20.435 (1) (a), the department may 6distribute epinephrine delivery systems to first responders who are trained in all 7subjects described under sub.
