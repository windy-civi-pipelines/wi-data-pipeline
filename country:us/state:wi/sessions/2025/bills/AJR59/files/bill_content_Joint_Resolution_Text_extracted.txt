Referred to Committee on Rules.AJR59,1,11Relating to: proclaiming June as Dairy Month in Wisconsin.AJR59,1,42Whereas, since 1937, the observation of Dairy Month in June has given 3Wisconsinites an opportunity to recognize the special role dairy has played in the 4state’s agricultural heritage; andAJR59,1,75Whereas, as early as the 1840s, dairy farmers from New York, then the 6nation’s leading dairy producer, migrated to Wisconsin and established a foothold 7for the dairy industry; andAJR59,1,108Whereas, in 1872, the Wisconsin Dairyman’s Association was founded with 9the goal of improving methods of making, selling, and marketing dairy products 10such as cheese and butter; andAJR59,1,1411Whereas, at the turn of the century, Wisconsin’s own Stephen Babcock 12developed a means to test the butterfat content of milk, which revolutionized the 13dairy industry by enabling standards of quality that could be used to establish milk 14prices; andAJR59,2,21Whereas, by the onset of World War I, Wisconsin surpassed all other states in 2dairy production; andAJR59,2,43Whereas, starting in 1940, Wisconsin cemented its reputation as “America’s 4Dairyland” by placing this slogan on license plates; andAJR59,2,65Whereas, today, over 1.28 million cows across nearly 6,000 dairy farms call 6Wisconsin home; andAJR59,2,87Whereas, the dairy industry contributes about $45.6 billion to the state 8economy annually; andAJR59,2,109Whereas, as a key part of this industry, Wisconsin cheesemakers produce over 10600 kinds of cheese and together produce over a quarter of the nation’s cheese; andAJR59,2,1311Whereas, because it has touched so many Wisconsinites’ lives, past and 12present, the dairy industry is a central component of Wisconsin heritage and 13identity; andAJR59,2,1614Whereas, in the month of June, the people of Wisconsin acknowledge the 15contributions of the state’s hardworking dairy farmers, who have made dairy a 16pillar of the state’s success; andAJR59,2,1817Whereas, these dairy farmers’ unwavering dedication to the highest standards 18of quality have consistently moved Wisconsin forward; now, therefore, be itAJR59,2,2019Resolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the legislature 20hereby proclaims June as Dairy Month in Wisconsin.AJR59,2,2121(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/ajr59 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/ajr59 proposaltext/2025/REG/AJR59 proposaltext/2025/REG/AJR59 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»AJR59: Joint Resolution Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
