Referred to Committee on Senate Organization.SJR71,1,11Relating to: honoring Jerry Apps for his contributions to Wisconsin’s heritage.SJR71,1,32Whereas, Jerry Apps became an active member of the Waushara County 4-H 3club at age 10 and has remained an advocate of 4-H throughout his life; andSJR71,1,64Whereas, Jerry Apps began his professional career as a county extension 5agent in Green Lake and Brown Counties, advising farm families and youth and 6serving as a fair organizer and judge; and SJR71,1,87Whereas, Jerry Apps taught thousands of students as a professor at the 8University of Wisconsin College of Agriculture; andSJR71,1,119Whereas, Jerry Apps has written more than 50 books on Wisconsin history 10and rural life, exploring topics such as barns, cheesemaking, gardening, fishing, 11conservation, horse-powered farming, one-room schools, and country winters; and SJR71,2,212Whereas, Jerry Apps has researched and published 23 books with the 13Wisconsin Historical Society Press, including the definitive Wisconsin Agriculture: 14A History, documenting in depth our state’s agricultural past, present, and future, 1as well as Old Farm: A History, which has won more than a dozen regional and 2national awards; andSJR71,2,43Whereas, Jerry Apps has written engaging and informative books for young 4readers that are enjoyed by schoolchildren in classrooms across Wisconsin; and SJR71,2,75Whereas, Jerry Apps has informed, entertained, and inspired countless 6residents of Wisconsin and the world through his newspaper columns, articles, 7books, and television and radio appearances; andSJR71,2,108Whereas, Jerry Apps is exceedingly generous with his knowledge and craft, 9teaching classes and workshops for aspiring writers and encouraging them to 10preserve and share their own stories; and SJR71,2,1411Whereas, Jerry Apps has earned dozens of writing awards; received the 12Distinguished Service Award from the UW–Madison College of Agricultural and 13Life Sciences; was named a fellow of the Wisconsin Academy of Sciences, Arts and 14Letters; and was inducted into the 4-H Hall of Fame; andSJR71,2,1715Whereas, Jerry Apps has created seven documentaries on rural life with PBS 16Wisconsin and won a regional Emmy Award for the documentary A Farm Winter, 17and two Eric Sevareid Awards for Merit for The Land and Food and Memories; andSJR71,2,2018Whereas, Jerry Apps stands alongside the great Wisconsin naturalist and 19environmental thinkers Aldo Leopold, John Muir, Sigurd Olson, Increase Lapham, 20and Gaylord Nelson; and SJR71,2,2321Whereas, Jerry Apps has shown unwavering dedication to preserving 22Wisconsin’s agricultural heritage and strengthening our bonds to our past and to 23one another; now, therefore, be itSJR71,3,224Resolved by the senate, the assembly concurring, That the Wisconsin 1Legislature honors Jerry Apps for his numerous, unique, and impactful 2contributions to Wisconsin’s heritage.SJR71,3,33(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/sjr71 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/sjr71 proposaltext/2025/REG/SJR71 proposaltext/2025/REG/SJR71 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»SJR71: Joint Resolution Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
