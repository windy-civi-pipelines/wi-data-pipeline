Referred to Committee on Mental Health, Substance Abuse Prevention, Children and Families.SB306,1,21An Act to create 36.25 (13h) of the statutes; relating to: provision of virtual 2mental health services for students at certain UW System institutions.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill requires the Board of Regents of the University of Wisconsin System to contract with a vendor for the provision of virtual mental health services for students enrolled in UW System institutions that have not more than 30,000 full-time enrolled undergraduate students, as such enrollment was counted on the preceding April 1.The bill also sets certain requirements for the vendor of the virtual mental health services, including that the vendor’s services be designed to complement existing institution-based mental health offerings and expand students’ access to mental health support services beyond traditional business hours. Additionally, the bill requires the vendor chosen by the board to annually report to the board and the legislature regarding the vendor’s provision of virtual mental health services, including certain statistics regarding students’ use of the vendor’s virtual mental health services.For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB306,11Section 1. 36.25 (13h) of the statutes is created to read:SB306,2,5236.25 (13h) Virtual mental health services. (a) The board shall contract 3with a vendor to provide virtual mental health services for students enrolled in 4institutions that have not more than 30,000 full-time enrolled undergraduate 5students, as such enrollment was counted on the preceding April 1.SB306,2,116(b) The virtual mental health services provided by the vendor contracted with 7under par. (a) shall be designed to complement existing institution-based mental 8health offerings, expand students’ access to mental health support services beyond 9traditional business hours, and include evidence-based clinical wellness content 10and behavioral health coaches and licensed mental health providers with 11experience in delivering mental health care to students in higher education.SB306,2,1612(c) In contracting with a vendor under par. (a) the board shall prioritize the 13selection of a vendor with at least 5 years of experience in providing mental health 14services to institutions and students in higher education and that has 15demonstrated experience in providing a software that engenders collaboration 16among the vendor and clinical staff present at an institution of higher education.SB306,3,217(d) A vendor contracted with under par. (a) shall, on July 1 of each year it 18provides virtual mental health services to students enrolled in institutions, submit 19to the board and to the chief clerk of each house of the legislature for distribution 20under s. 13.172 (3) to the standing committees having jurisdiction over higher 21education matters a report regarding the vendor’s provision of virtual mental 22health services.
