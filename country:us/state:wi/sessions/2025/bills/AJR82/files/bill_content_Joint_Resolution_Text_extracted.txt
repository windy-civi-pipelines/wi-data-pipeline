andAJR82,1,54Whereas, Hispanics and Latinos contribute to the economy, arts, sciences, 5sports, and culture in the United States; andAJR82,1,76Whereas, Latinos serve in government at every level, from school boards to the 7state house, from city councils and mayoral offices to the U.S. Congress; andAJR82,1,1313Whereas, about 450,000 Latinos call Wisconsin home; andAJR82,2,214Whereas, this figure includes JoCasta Zamarripa, who became the first 1Latina elected to the Wisconsin Legislature in November 2010 and later became a 2member of the Milwaukee Common Council; andAJR82,2,53Whereas, this figure also includes Judge Pedro Colón, who was the first Latino 4elected to the Wisconsin Legislature in November 1998 and was sworn in as the 5state’s first Latino appellate court judge in November 2023; now, therefore, be itAJR82,2,96Resolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the members of 7the Wisconsin Legislature honor the achievements of Hispanics and Latinos in 8America and honor the many ways they continue to enrich our nation and shape our 9common future;
