andWhereas, the construction industry is one of Wisconsin’s largest industries, with nearly 6 percent of the Wisconsin workforce being employed in construction; andWhereas, construction is a true opportunity career, allowing anyone to start as a general laborer, become an apprentice, develop a specialization, gain licensing and credentials, advance into a supervisory role, and eventually become their own boss; andWhereas, construction is a lifelong career where you can raise a family in a career that cannot be outsourced; andWhereas, Wisconsin’s world class apprenticeship program allows individuals to earn when they learn without accumulating overwhelming student debt; andWhereas, our fellow Wisconsinites work every day in construction, building the houses we live in, the schools we learn in, the hospitals we are healed in, and the houses of worship we pray in; andWhereas, in Wisconsin, the construction industry needs nearly 500,000 new craft professionals in 2026, according to Associated Builders and Contractors; andWhereas, we are pleased to honor construction craft professionals and the critical role they play in the development and growth of our state; andWhereas, during this month of October, employers, associations, and schools are encouraged to conduct job fairs, panel discussions, and local community events to inform students of the vast employment opportunities in construction that lead to rewarding, long-term careers; andWhereas, this month, the state of Wisconsin joins dedicated individuals and organizations across our state in honoring our state’s construction craft professionals for their hard work and dedication and recognizing the critical role they play in the continued development and maintenance of our state; now, therefore, be itResolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the Wisconsin State Legislature proclaims October 2025 as Careers in Construction Month in Wisconsin.
