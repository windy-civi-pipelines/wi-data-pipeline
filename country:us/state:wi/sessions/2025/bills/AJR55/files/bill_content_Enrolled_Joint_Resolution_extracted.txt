andWhereas, mental health conditions affect one in five U.S. adults and one in six youth annually, touching the lives of Wisconsinites across every age group and region; andWhereas, Wisconsin is facing a significant rise in youth mental health challenges, mirroring a nationwide 31 percent increase in emergency room visits related to mental health among young people in 2020, a concerning trend that has continued to grow in recent years; andWhereas, in both rural and urban settings, access to professional mental health services across all 72 Wisconsin counties remains essential for meeting the mental health needs of Wisconsin’s population; andWhereas, by providing care, guidance, and connection, mental health professionals and peer support networks empower individuals to manage mental health challenges and work toward a brighter, healthier future; andWhereas, with effective treatment, education, and community support, individuals experiencing mental health conditions can recover, grow, and thrive; andWhereas, the Wisconsin Legislature recognizes the critical need to strengthen mental health systems and remains committed to advancing initiatives that expand access, reduce barriers, and improve awareness; now, therefore, be itResolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the Wisconsin Legislature designates May 2025 and May 2026 as Mental Health Awareness Months in Wisconsin.Senator Mary FelzkowskiPresident of the SenateRepresentative Robin J.
