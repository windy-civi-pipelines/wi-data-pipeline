Referred to Committee on Government Operations, Labor and Economic Development.SB362,1,21An Act to create 5.02 (6m) (a) 5. of the statutes; relating to: forms of proof of 2identification for voting.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauCurrent law requires an individual who is a qualified elector to provide proof of identification to vote. The document that the individual presents for proof of identification must contain the individual’s name and a photograph of the individual. Documents that are considered proof of identification are enumerated in statute and include a U.S. passport, an identification card issued by a U.S. uniformed service, an operator’s license issued by the Department of Transportation, and an identification card issued by DOT.This bill adds to the list of documents that may be used as proof of identification for voting a valid operator’s license or identification card that satisfies federal REAL ID requirements and is issued by a state of the United States, the District of Columbia, Puerto Rico, the Virgin Islands, Guam, American Samoa, the Northern Mariana Islands, the Trust Territory of the Pacific Islands, or any other territory or possession of the United States.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB362,13Section 1. 5.02 (6m) (a) 5. of the statutes is created to read:SB362,2,315.02 (6m) (a) 5. An operator’s license or identification card issued by any 2state, as defined in P.L. 109-13, section 201 (5), that satisfies the requirements of 3P.L. 109-13, section 202.SB362,2,44(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/sb362 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/sb362 proposaltext/2025/REG/SB362 proposaltext/2025/REG/SB362 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»SB362: Bill Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
