2025 SENATE BILL 187
April 14, 2025 - Introduced by Senators L. Johnson, Drake, Hesselbein, Larson, Spreitzer and Wirch, cosponsored by Representatives Madison, Haywood, Moore Omokunde, Arney, Goodwin, Rivera-Wagner, Stubbs, Taylor, Bare, Brown, Clancy, DeSmidt, Fitzgerald, Hong, Joers, Kirsch, Miresse, Phelps, Prado, Roe, Tenorio and Udell. Referred to Committee on Government Operations, Labor and Economic Development.
SB187,1,2
1An Act relating to: study of guaranteed employment grant program and
2making an appropriation.
Analysis by the Legislative Reference Bureau
This bill requires the Department of Workforce Development to study the creation of a job guarantee and full employment grant program. Under the program, DWD and municipalities provide grants to public and nonprofit employers in the care economy to provide certain job opportunities in positions covered by a collective bargaining unit to individuals with disabilities, heads of households with incomes that are 150 percent or less than the federal poverty line, individuals who have been unemployed for extended periods, individuals who were previously incarcerated, and individuals between 18 and 24 years old. The bill requires DWD to submit a report with the findings and recommendations.
For further information see the state fiscal estimate, which will be printed as an appendix to this bill.
The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:

1(1) Job guarantee and full employment grant study. The department of
2workforce development shall conduct a study of the requirements to create a grant

5create and offer guaranteed jobs in positions that are covered by a collective
6bargaining agreement, for at least 100 days of 8 hours per day at a pay rate of $20
7per hour. In this subsection, “care economy” means job sectors that prioritize care
8for people, communities, and the environment, and includes health care, child care,
9elder and disability care, social services, public transportation, sustainability,
10conservation or climate change mitigation, public infrastructure, education, public
11and social housing, and criminal legal reform. The study shall determine the
12number of positions required, the cost, the number of individuals who would be
13eligible for a position under the grant program, paid training strategies for jobs
14offered under the program, and strategies to create pathways to and opportunities
15for full-time employment. Eligible employers would be able to use grant money to
16create and offer jobs in 25-day increments to any of the following:
SB187,2,1717(a) Individuals between 18 and 24 years old.
SB187,2,1818(b) Individuals with a disability.
SB187,2,2119(c) Heads of households whose household income is 150 percent or less of the
20nonfarm federal poverty line for the continental United States, as defined in 42
21USC 9902 (2).
SB187,3,322(d) Individuals who have been unemployed and searching for a job for 27
23weeks or more, or who have been unemployed for 3 of the preceding 5 years, except
1that employers of such individuals would no longer be eligible for a grant under the
2program for individuals who have been employed by the same employer for more
3than 12 consecutive months.
SB187,3,44(e) Individuals who were previously incarcerated.
SB187,3,105(2) Job guarantee and full employment report. No later than 18 months
6after the effective date of this subsection, the department of workforce development
7shall submit a report with its written findings and recommendations resulting
8from the study under sub. (1) to the governor and to the chief clerk of each house of
9the legislature for distribution to the appropriate standing committees in the
10manner provided under s. 13.172 (3).

SB187,3,1612(1) Job guarantee and full employment grant study. In the schedule
