2025 ASSEMBLY JOINT RESOLUTION 85
August 29, 2025 - Introduced by Representatives Brown, Arney, DeSmidt, Emerson, Fitzgerald, Johnson, McCarville, Miresse, Murphy, Mursau, Prado, Roe, Sheehan, Sinicki, Stroud, Stubbs, Tenorio and Udell, cosponsored by Senators Spreitzer, Cabral-Guevara, Drake, Hesselbein, Larson, Marklein, Ratcliff and Roys. Referred to Committee on Rules.
AJR85,1,2
1Relating to: congratulating the University of Wisconsin-Whitewater Baseball
2Team on winning the 2025 NCAA Division III National Championship.
AJR85,1,53Whereas, the University of Wisconsin-Whitewater Baseball Team won a
4record-breaking third title after securing the 2025 NCAA Division III National
5Championship on June 4, 2025; and
AJR85,1,106Whereas, after a historic tournament run, the UW-Whitewater Baseball Team
7defeated Messiah University 21-5 in a best-of-three series, which the team swept,
8setting a new DIII record with 74 runs scored over the course of the tournament
9and finishing the season with 49 wins and 6 losses—the most in program history;
10and
AJR85,1,1211Whereas, the Warhawks scored double-digit runs in four out of five of the
12College World Series tournament games in the 2025 season; and
AJR85,2,213Whereas, during the final game, Junior Andy Thies hit a three-run home run
14in the top of the first inning, Sophomore Aaron Holland hit an RBI single to the left
1in the top of the second inning, and Senior Darryl Jackson hit a single at the top of
2the fourth inning; and
AJR85,2,43Whereas, Senior Matt Scolan registered his 300th career hit on a two-run
4home run in the sixth inning; and
AJR85,2,65Whereas, Junior Cade Hansen pitched 5.1 innings, struck out four, issued no
6walks, and allowed only two runs on three hits; and
AJR85,2,107Whereas, Senior Adam Cootway was named Most Outstanding Player of the
8tournament, was responsible for 18 Warhawk runs, hit four home runs over the
9course of the tournament, and set multiple records in the eight-team College World
10Series with 12 runs, 34 total bases, and two triples; and
AJR85,2,2311Whereas, the team was led by 2025 DIII Baseball All-Tournament Team
12members Senior Adam Cootway, Senior Eli Frank, Junior Andy Thies, Junior
13Danny Hopper, Senior Sam Paden, Senior Matt Scolan, Junior Cade Hansen, and
14Sophomore Ben Lee, who were joined by teammates Senior Charlie Bogue, Junior
15Brandon Brust, Junior Sava Dupor, Junior Logan Eisenbarth, Junior Reese
16Fetherston, Sophomore Jack Hagen, Sophomore George Hansen, Sophomore Wyatt
17Hansen, Freshman Maddux Hibbard, Sophomore Aaron Holland, Sophomore Cade
18Honkamp, Senior Max Huseboe, Senior Darryl Jackson, Junior Wyatt Jensen,
19Sophomore Jackson Koenig, Sophomore Brady Malkow, Sophomore Dominik
20McVay, Senior Alex Najera, Freshman Evan Olson, Junior Cooper Pease, Freshman
21Brett Sommer, Freshman Jackson Spring, Sophomore Jack Taczy, Freshman
22Hudson Turner, Junior Austin Wessel, Sophomore Ethan Wickman, and Junior
23Ryne Willard; and
AJR85,3,924Whereas, the team was managed by dedicated coaches and staff, led by Head
1Coach John Vodenlich in his 22nd season with UW-Whitewater, Assistant Coach
2Zach Bayreuther in his second season with UW-Whitewater, Assistant Coach
3Peyton Schneider in his second season with UW-Whitewater, Assistant Coach
4Armando Hernandez in his 28th season with UW-Whitewater, Assistant Coach
5Tom Klawitter in his ninth season with UW-Whitewater, Assistant Coach Mark
6Fuller in his 12th season with UW-Whitewater, Director of Baseball Operations
7Sam Martin in his second season with UW-Whitewater, Athletic Trainer Kelly
8Loudon in her second season with UW-Whitewater, and Director of Sports
9Performance Lee Munger in his 20th season with UW-Whitewater; and
AJR85,3,1210Whereas, Head Coach John Vodenlich celebrated his 800th career win this
11season, and the team has not finished with a losing record since he took over the
12program in 2004; and
AJR85,3,1513Whereas, 2025 was the 10th College World Series appearance in program
14history and the third NCAA DIII National Championship win for the UW-
15Whitewater Baseball program, with prior wins in 2005 and 2014; and
AJR85,3,1816Whereas, the UW-Whitewater community and our state take pride in the
17integrity, leadership, and excellence the team has demonstrated during this
18historic season; now, therefore, be it
AJR85,4,219Resolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the members of
20the Wisconsin State Legislature do hereby congratulate and commend the
21University of Wisconsin-Whitewater Baseball Team, coaching staff, and athletic
22department on winning the 2025 NCAA Division III National Championship title
1and wish the student-athletes, coaches, and staff continued success on and off the
2field.
AJR85,4,33(end)
