Referred to Committee on Licensing, Regulatory Reform, State and Federal Affairs.SB40,1,31An Act to create 1.14 (1m) of the statutes; relating to: flags flown, hung, or 2displayed from a flagpole or the exterior of state and local buildings and 3eliminating a related administrative rule.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill prohibits, with certain exceptions, any flag other than the U.S. flag and the flag of the state of Wisconsin from being flown, hung, or displayed from a flagpole or the exterior of any state office building or facility, including the state capitol, or from any local government building or school building. The bill also repeals an administrative rule that includes a similar requirement but authorizes the governor to direct otherwise.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB40,14Section 1. 1.14 (1m) of the statutes is created to read:SB40,2,551.14 (1m) (a) The U.S. flag, the flag of the state of Wisconsin, and any flag of 6a state agency shall be the only flags flown, hung, or displayed from a flagpole or 1the exterior of any state office building or facility, including the state capitol, from a 2flagpole or the exterior of any building or facility leased by a state agency, from a 3flagpole or the exterior of any building or facility owned or leased by a local 4governmental unit, or from a flagpole or the exterior of any building or facility 5owned, leased, or used by a school board.SB40,2,86(b) Paragraph (a) does not apply to any of the following, if the flag is flown, 7hung, or displayed from a flagpole or the exterior of a public building or facility 8described in par. (a), in accordance with 4 USC 7:SB40,2,1091. A prisoners of war or missing in action flag or other appropriate flag 10commemorating veterans, the U.S. or state armed forces, or the military.SB40,2,12112. A flag of a federally recognized American Indian tribe or band for special, 12occasional, or ceremonial purposes.SB40,2,14133. A flag of a state or territory of the United States, for special, occasional, or 14ceremonial purposes.SB40,2,15154. A flag of a foreign nation for special, occasional, or ceremonial purposes.SB40,2,17165. A flag of a foreign nation to identify persons officially representing such 17foreign nation or the property or premises of the person or nation.SB40,2,18186. A flag of a local governmental unit.SB40,2,20197. A recognized flag of a unit of firefighters, law enforcement officers, 20emergency medical technicians, or other first responders.SB40,2,22218. A flag of a state that borders Wisconsin, at a shared weighing station at a 22port of entry.SB40,3,219. A flag flown, hung, or displayed at a marina, airport, airfield, train, or bus 2terminal, if the purpose of the flag at the site is for safety.SB40,3,4310. An official flag of a school or university or mascot, or the official flags of 4visiting schools and universities for special, occasional, or ceremonial purposes.SB40,3,125(c) No flag flown, hung, or displayed under an exception in par. (b) may be a 6flag that has been used for or is used for ceremonial purposes related to a political 7party, a political or social cause, a sexual orientation or gender identity, a political, 8ideological, or religious viewpoint, or a gang or extremist group as designated by the 9federal bureau of investigation, nor a flag that commemorates, represents, or shows 10support for or opposition to a political party, a political or social cause, a racial 11identity, a sexual orientation or gender identity, or a political, ideological, or 12religious viewpoint.SB40,3,1713(d) No flag flown, hung, or displayed under an exception in par. (b) may be a 14flag that has been used for or is used for ceremonial purposes and was designed to 15recognize a holiday listed under s. 230.35 (4) (a) or 995.20 nor a flag that represents, 16commemorates, or has been designed to recognize a holiday listed under s. 230.35 17(4) (a) or 995.20. SB40,3,2018(e) Paragraph (a) does not affect the rights of a federally recognized American 19Indian tribe or band to fly, hang, or display flags on property within the boundaries 20of an Indian reservation or other land held in trust for the tribe or band.SB40,3,2221(f) Paragraph (a) does not apply to a state office building, facility, or property 22that is leased or rented to a private, nongovernmental entity.SB40,223Section 2. Adm 2.07 (1) of the administrative code is repealed.SB40,3,2424(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/sb40 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/sb40 proposaltext/2025/REG/SB40 proposaltext/2025/REG/SB40 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»SB40: Bill Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
