Referred to Committee on Insurance, Housing, Rural Issues and Forestry.SB342,1,31An Act to create 609.865 and 632.895 (12g) of the statutes; relating to: 2coverage of treatment for mental health or substance use disorders under 3health insurance policies and plans.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill requires health insurance policies and self-insured governmental health plans that provide coverage of mental health or behavioral health items or services to cover in each policy year at least 28 appointments or visits with a mental health care provider to treat mental health or substance use disorders or as many appointments or visits with a mental health care provider as are necessary to meet the insured’s treatment goals. The bill prohibits health insurance policies and self-insured governmental health plans from requiring prior authorization for the coverage of appointments or visits under the bill. Health insurance policies are known as disability insurance policies in the bill. Further, this bill requires the Office of the Commissioner of Insurance to prepare a preliminary actuarial estimate of the average cost for all qualified health plans, as defined under federal law, attributable to the coverage required under the bill. If the preliminary actuarial estimate of the average cost for all qualified health plans is an increase of greater than 10 percent, OCI may not enforce the coverage requirements under the bill.This proposal may contain a health insurance mandate requiring a social and financial impact report under s. 601.423, stats.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB342,11Section 1. 609.865 of the statutes is created to read:SB342,2,42609.865 Coverage of treatment for mental health or substance use 3disorders.
