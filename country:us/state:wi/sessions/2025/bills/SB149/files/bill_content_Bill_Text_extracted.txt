Referred to Committee on Government Operations, Labor and Economic Development.SB149,1,31An Act to create 13.40 of the statutes; relating to: requiring the legislature to 2convene an extraordinary session if an executive order of the president of the 3United States freezes federal aid to the state.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill requires the legislature to convene an extraordinary session if the president of the United States issues an executive order under which federal aid to Wisconsin that was previously authorized under an act of Congress is frozen. The extraordinary session must be called for legislative consideration of a bill that does all of the following:1. Establishes a procedure to estimate the amounts of federal aid that each state agency, eligible local governmental unit, and eligible nonprofit organization is likely to be deprived of as a result of the executive order. Only those local governmental units and nonprofit organizations that receive federal moneys under a state appropriation qualify as eligible.2. Provides for payments from the budget stabilization fund to state agencies, eligible local governmental units, and eligible nonprofit organizations to offset estimated deprivation amounts.3. Prioritizes payments to offset deprivation amounts of federal aid that fund essential services.4. Limits payments to eligible local governmental units and eligible nonprofit organizations to no more than the amounts the eligible local governmental unit or eligible nonprofit organization would have received under a state appropriation of federal moneys but for the executive order.5. To the extent allowed under federal law, provides for reimbursement to the budget stabilization fund for the payments specified in item 2 whenever the state receives a back payment of federal aid moneys that were previously frozen by executive order of the president.6. Provides that if, in lieu of reimbursement to the budget stabilization fund, federal aid moneys received by the state as a back payment are paid to a state agency, eligible local governmental unit, or eligible nonprofit organization, the state agency, eligible local governmental unit, or eligible nonprofit organization must remit to the state for deposit in the budget stabilization fund an amount equal to the back payment amount received.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB149,11Section 1. 13.40 of the statutes is created to read:SB149,2,2213.40 Frozen federal aid. (1) Definitions. In this section:SB149,2,73(a) “Eligible local governmental unit” means a political subdivision, a special 4purpose district, an agency or corporation of a political subdivision or special 5purpose district, a school district, or a combination or subunit of any of the 6foregoing, in this state that receives funding under an appropriation of federal 7moneys made under ch. 20.SB149,2,118(b) “Eligible nonprofit organization” means an organization or association not 9organized or conducted for pecuniary profit that is exempt from taxation under 10section 501 (c) of the Internal Revenue Code and that receives funding under an 11appropriation of federal moneys made under ch. 20.SB149,3,212(c) “State agency” means an association, authority, board, department, 13commission, independent agency, institution, system, office, society, or other body 1in state government created or authorized to be created by the constitution or any 2law.SB149,3,93(2) Extraordinary session. If the president of the United States issues an 4executive order under which federal aid to this state that was previously authorized 5under an act of Congress is frozen, the assembly and senate committees on 6organization shall as soon as practicable and without delay convene an 7extraordinary session of the legislature for legislative consideration of a bill, which 8the assembly or senate committee on organization shall have drafted and 9introduced, that does all of the following:SB149,3,1210(a) Establishes a procedure to estimate the amounts of federal aid that each 11state agency, eligible local governmental unit, and eligible nonprofit organization is 12likely to be deprived of as a result of the executive order.SB149,3,1513(b) Provides for payments from the budget stabilization fund to state agencies, 14eligible local governmental units, and eligible nonprofit organizations to offset 15deprivation amounts estimated under the procedure established in par. (a).SB149,3,1816(c) Prioritizes payments under par. (b) to offset estimated deprivation 17amounts of federal aid that fund essential services, which shall be defined under 18the bill.SB149,3,2219(d) Limits payments to eligible local governmental units and eligible nonprofit 20organizations to no more than the amounts the eligible local governmental unit or 21eligible nonprofit organization would have received under an appropriation of 22federal moneys made under ch. 20 but for the executive order.SB149,4,223(e) To the extent allowed under federal law, provides for reimbursement to the 24budget stabilization fund for payments made under par. (b) whenever the state 1receives a back payment of federal aid moneys that were previously frozen by 2executive order of the president.SB149,4,93(f) Provides that if, in lieu of reimbursement to the budget stabilization fund 4under par. (e), federal aid moneys received by the state as a back payment are paid 5to a state agency, eligible local governmental unit, or eligible nonprofit 6organization, the state agency, eligible local governmental unit, or eligible nonprofit 7organization shall remit to the state for deposit in the budget stabilization fund an 8amount equal to the back payment amount previously paid to the state agency, 9eligible local governmental unit, or eligible nonprofit organization.SB149,4,1010(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/sb149 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/sb149 proposaltext/2025/REG/SB149 proposaltext/2025/REG/SB149 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»SB149: Bill Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
