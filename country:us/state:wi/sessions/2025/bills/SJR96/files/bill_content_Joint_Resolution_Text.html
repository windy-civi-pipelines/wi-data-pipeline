2025 SENATE JOINT RESOLUTION 96
October 9, 2025 - Introduced by Senators James, Cabral-Guevara, Hutton, Jacque, Marklein, Ratcliff, Spreitzer, Wall and Roys, cosponsored by Representatives Franklin, Allen, Anderson, Bare, Behnke, Billings, Goeben, Gundrum, B. Jacobson, Kaufert, Knodl, Kreibich, Kurtz, Melotik, Miresse, Murphy, Mursau, Nedweski, Novak, Palmeri, Penterman, Rivera-Wagner, Tranel, Wichgers, Andraca and Johnson. Referred to Committee on Senate Organization.
SJR96,1,1
1Relating to: proclaiming October 2025 as Careers in Construction Month.
SJR96,1,42Whereas, Careers in Construction Month is designated annually to increase
3public awareness and appreciation of construction craft professionals and the entire
4construction workforce; and
SJR96,1,75Whereas, the construction industry is one of Wisconsin’s largest industries,
6with nearly 6 percent of the Wisconsin workforce being employed in construction;
7and
SJR96,1,118Whereas, construction is a true opportunity career, allowing anyone to start as
9a general laborer, become an apprentice, develop a specialization, gain licensing and
10credentials, advance into a supervisory role, and eventually become their own boss;
11and
SJR96,1,1312Whereas, construction is a lifelong career where you can raise a family in a
13career that cannot be outsourced; and
SJR96,2,2
1Whereas, Wisconsin’s world class apprenticeship program allows individuals
2to earn when they learn without accumulating overwhelming student debt; and
SJR96,2,53Whereas, our fellow Wisconsinites work every day in construction, building
4the houses we live in, the schools we learn in, the hospitals we are healed in, and the
5houses of worship we pray in; and
SJR96,2,76Whereas, in Wisconsin, the construction industry needs nearly 500,000 new
7craft professionals in 2026, according to Associated Builders and Contractors; and
SJR96,2,98Whereas, we are pleased to honor construction craft professionals and the
9critical role they play in the development and growth of our state; and
SJR96,2,1310Whereas, during this month of October, employers, associations, and schools
11are encouraged to conduct job fairs, panel discussions, and local community events
12to inform students of the vast employment opportunities in construction that lead
13to rewarding, long-term careers; and
SJR96,2,1814Whereas, this month, the state of Wisconsin joins dedicated individuals and
15organizations across our state in honoring our state’s construction craft
16professionals for their hard work and dedication and recognizing the critical role
17they play in the continued development and maintenance of our state; now,
18therefore, be it
SJR96,2,2119Resolved by the senate, the assembly concurring, That the Wisconsin
20State Legislature proclaims October 2025 as Careers in Construction Month in
21Wisconsin.
SJR96,2,2222(end)
