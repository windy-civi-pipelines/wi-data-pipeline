Referred to Committee on Rules.AJR86,1,11Relating to: honoring the life and public service of Representative Jim Rooney.AJR86,1,42Whereas, Representative James “Jim” Francis Rooney, a lifelong public 3servant, honorable veteran, and devoted husband, father, and grandfather, passed 4away on May 3, 2025, at the age of 89; andAJR86,1,65Whereas, Jim Rooney was born on September 29, 1935, in Racine, Wisconsin, 6the youngest of seven children of Cornelius and Mabel Rooney; andAJR86,1,87Whereas, Jim Rooney graduated from St. Catherine’s High School in 1953 and 8proudly served in the U.S. Army from 1954 to 1956; andAJR86,1,119Whereas, Jim Rooney began his professional career at Nielsen Madsen & 10Barber, where he worked as a field surveyor and business manager before 11embarking on a distinguished public service career; andAJR86,2,212Whereas, Jim Rooney served on the Racine County Board from 1966 to 1984 13and from 1998 to 2000, including as vice chair from 1980 to 1982 and chair from 11982 to 1984, and demonstrated an enduring commitment to the people of Racine 2County; andAJR86,2,83Whereas, Jim Rooney also served his community as chairman of the Racine 4Board of Harbor Commissioners, Racine County highway commissioner, and 5Racine County public works director, while also contributing to regional policy 6through his work with the Southeast Wisconsin Regional Planning Commission, 7Regional Transportation Authority Commission, and Racine Heritage Museum 8Board; andAJR86,2,109Whereas, Jim Rooney was elected to the Wisconsin State Assembly in 1972 10and served until 1985; and AJR86,2,1511Whereas, during his tenure in the legislature, Jim Rooney served as caucus 12chair for the assembly Democrats in four legislative sessions; held leadership 13positions on key committees, including those on elections, highways, and banking; 14and contributed to the Governor’s Task Force on County Government Organization 15and Administration; andAJR86,2,1716Whereas, Jim Rooney authored Chapter 274, Laws of 1977, which created the 17Wisconsin Waterways Commission; andAJR86,2,2118Whereas, Jim Rooney served as chair of the Wisconsin Waterways 19Commission from 1985 to 2019 under both Republican and Democratic governors, 20during which time he advocated for marina and waterway improvements across the 21state; and AJR86,2,2422Whereas, Jim Rooney was a devoted sailor and a longtime member of the 23Racine Yacht Club, where he founded the club’s junior program in 1947 and served 24as commodore in 1966; andAJR86,3,41Whereas, Jim Rooney played a pivotal role in transforming Racine’s lakefront 2by working with the federal government to decommission a commercial port and 3develop Reefpoint Marina, sparking a yearslong revitalization that reshaped 4downtown Racine and the county’s eastern shoreline for generations to enjoy; andAJR86,3,85Whereas, Jim Rooney was a devoted husband to his wife of 65 years, Nancy; a 6loving father to his children Erin, James, John, and Richard; and a proud 7grandfather to Jonathon, Ryan, Sarah, Andrew, Matthew, Connell, Katelyn, and 8Colleen; andAJR86,3,129Whereas, Jim Rooney’s sharp wit, warm heart, deep Irish pride, spirited 10holiday traditions, and unwavering commitment to Racine County and the State of 11Wisconsin will be remembered and missed by all who knew him; now, therefore, be 12itAJR86,3,1513Resolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the members of 14the Wisconsin Legislature honor the life and public service of Representative Jim 15Rooney and extend their condolences to his family and friends.AJR86,3,1616(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/ajr86 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/ajr86 proposaltext/2025/REG/AJR86 proposaltext/2025/REG/AJR86 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»AJR86: Joint Resolution Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
