andWhereas, first responders include all professional and volunteer police, fire, and emergency medical services personnel; andWhereas, first responders risk their safety on a daily basis to protect the public from threats and emergency situations and provide lifesaving services; andWhereas, first responders stand ready to serve and protect the public 24 hours per day; andWhereas, there are approximately 4.6 million first responders in the United States serving in disciplines such as law enforcement, emergency management, emergency medical services, fire and rescue, and public works; andWhereas, in Wisconsin, there are over 4,880 career firefighters, 20,600 volunteer firefighters, 14,700 law enforcement officers, and 17,426 career and volunteer emergency medical services personnel; andWhereas, first responders are vital to ensuring safety and security in our nation, state, and communities and deserve to be honored and recognized for their commitment to providing safety and lifesaving services; now, therefore, be itResolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the Wisconsin Legislature recognizes October 14, 2025, as First Responders Appreciation Day.
