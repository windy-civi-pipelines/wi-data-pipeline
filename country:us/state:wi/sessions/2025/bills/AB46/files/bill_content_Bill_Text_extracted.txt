Referred to Committee on Local Government.AB46,1,31An Act to create 1.14 (1) (am) and 1.14 (1m) of the statutes; relating to: flags 2flown at state and local government buildings and eliminating a related 3administrative rule.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauThis bill prohibits any flag other than the U.S. flag, the state flag, the official POW/MIA flag recognized by Congress, and the official flags of each branch of the U.S. armed forces from being flown, hung, or displayed from any flagpole or building, structure, or facility, including the state capitol, that is owned or occupied entirely by a state agency or authority or by a city, village, town, or county. However, the bill authorizes the legislature to make exceptions for state flagpoles and facilities by joint resolution adopted by a three-fourths majority vote of all members elected to each house. Similarly, a city, village, town, or county may make exceptions for its flagpoles and facilities by a three-fourths majority vote of all members elected to its governing body.The bill also repeals an administrative rule that includes requirements similar to those of the bill but that authorizes the governor to make exceptions. The bill delays its requirements for state flagpoles and facilities until January 1, 2027.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB46,11Section 1. 1.14 (1) (am) of the statutes is created to read:AB46,2,221.14 (1) (am) “Political subdivision” means any city, village, town, or county.AB46,23Section 2. 1.14 (1m) of the statutes is created to read:AB46,2,1041.14 (1m) (a) The U.S. flag, the state flag, the official POW/MIA flag 5recognized by Congress under P.L. 101-355, and the official flags of each branch of 6the U.S. armed forces shall be the only flags flown, hung, or displayed from any 7flagpole or building, structure, or facility, including the state capitol, that is owned 8or occupied entirely by a state agency or state authority, except that the legislature 9may direct otherwise by joint resolution adopted by a three-fourths majority vote of 10all members elected to each house.AB46,2,1611(b) The U.S. flag, the state flag, the official POW/MIA flag recognized by 12Congress under P.L. 101-355, and the official flags of each branch of the U.S. armed 13forces shall be the only flags flown, hung, or displayed from any flagpole or building, 14structure, or facility that is owned or occupied entirely by a political subdivision, 15except that a political subdivision may direct otherwise by a three-fourths majority 16vote of the members elect of its governing body.AB46,317Section 3. Adm 2.07 (1) of the administrative code is repealed.AB46,418Section 4. Initial applicability.AB46,2,2019(1) State facilities. The treatment of s. 1.14 (1m) (a) first applies to flags 20flown, hung, or displayed on January 1, 2027.AB46,521Section 5. Effective dates. This act takes effect on the day after 22publication, except as follows:AB46,3,21(1) Notwithstanding s. 227.265, the treatment of administrative rules takes 2effect on January 1, 2027.AB46,3,33(end) Down Down /2025/related/proposals/ab46 true proposaltext /2025/related/proposals/ab46 proposaltext/2025/REG/AB46 proposaltext/2025/REG/AB46 section true Menu»2025»Related Documents»Proposal Text»AB46: Bill Text × Details for PDF view Link (Permanent link) Bookmark this location View toggle Go to top of document Search in this chapter Search in this section Search in this agency Search in this chapter group Search in this chapter Search in this section Cross references for section Acts affecting this section References to this 1970 Statutes Annotations Appellate Court Citations Administrative Code Index Reference lines Clear highlighting
