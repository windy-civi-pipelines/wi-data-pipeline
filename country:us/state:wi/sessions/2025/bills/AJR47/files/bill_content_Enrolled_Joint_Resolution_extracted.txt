andWhereas, millions of individuals worldwide experience vision impairment or blindness, many of which cases could be prevented or treated with proper awareness, early detection, and access to care; andWhereas, eye health conditions such as amblyopia, cataracts, glaucoma, macular degeneration, and refractive errors continue to affect individuals of all ages, underscoring the need for education and proactive measures; andWhereas, the month of May provides an opportunity to raise public awareness about the importance of protecting eyesight, promoting regular eye exams, and supporting research and initiatives to improve eye health; andWhereas, organizations, health care professionals, and communities are committed to reducing vision loss through prevention, treatment, and advocacy efforts; now, therefore, be itResolved by the assembly, the senate concurring, That the Wisconsin Legislature hereby proclaims May as Eye Health Awareness Month; and, be it furtherResolved, That the Wisconsin Legislature encourages all residents to prioritize their eye health by scheduling regular eye exams, adopting protective measures such as wearing sunglasses, and learning about the risks and symptoms of eye conditions; and, be it furtherResolved, That the Wisconsin Legislature calls upon public and private entities to join in this observance by promoting education and providing resources.Senator Mary FelzkowskiPresident of the SenateRepresentative Robin J.
