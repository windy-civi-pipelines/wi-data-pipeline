Referred to Committee on State Affairs. ***AUTHORS SUBJECT TO CHANGE***AB572,1,21An Act to amend 563.03 (1m) of the statutes; relating to: the definition of 2bingo.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauUnder current law, “bingo” is generally defined as a game of chance in which players pay a consideration in order to participate via a standardized card and in which winners are determined and prizes awarded in a certain way. The definition of “bingo” does not include a game using free cards and donated prizes, if any, for which no payment of consideration is made by participants.This bill provides that “bingo” does not include a game using free cards and donated prizes, if any, for which no payment of consideration is made by participants, regardless of whether the host or sponsor of the game pays consideration to another person in exchange for the prizes, supplies, equipment, or logistical support for the game.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:AB572,13Section 1. 563.03 (1m) of the statutes is amended to read:AB572,2,134563.03 (1m) “Bingo” means a game of chance in which players pay a 1consideration in order to participate, in which 75 numbered objects are available for 2selection, and those objects actually selected are selected on a random basis, and in 3which each player participates by means of cards sold, rented or used only at the 4time and place of the game, each card containing 5 rows of 5 spaces each, each space 5printed with a number from 1 to 75, except the central space, which is marked 6“FREE”.
