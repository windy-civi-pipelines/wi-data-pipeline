Referred to Committee on Senate Organization.SJR92,1,21Relating to: proclaiming October 2025 as Filipino American History Month in the 2state of Wisconsin.SJR92,1,53Whereas, the first documented landing of Filipinos in the continental United 4States occurred in October 1587, when the Manila-built galleon ship Nuestra 5Señora de Esperanza arrived at what is now Morro Bay, California; andSJR92,1,86Whereas, the Filipino American National Historical Society recognizes 1763 7as the year in which the first permanent Filipino settlement in the United States 8was established, in St. Malo, Louisiana; andSJR92,1,119Whereas, the Filipino American community is the third-largest Asian 10American and Pacific Islander group in the United States, with a population of 11approximately 4,600,000 people; andSJR92,1,1212Whereas, more than 22,000 Wisconsinites identify as Filipino; andSJR92,2,213Whereas, since 2000, the Filipino American community has grown by 78 1percent, making it one of the fastest-growing Asian American groups in the United 2States; andSJR92,2,53Whereas, Filipino Americans have contributed greatly to the cultural, 4political, social, and economic prosperity of the United States and the state of 5Wisconsin; andSJR92,2,96Whereas, Filipinos and Filipino Americans have a longstanding history of 7serving in the U.S. Armed Forces, from the Civil War to the Iraq and Afghanistan 8conflicts, including 250,000 Filipinos who fought alongside American soldiers 9during World War II; andSJR92,2,1310Whereas, Filipino Americans have played an integral role in countless fields 11that enrich the United States, including agriculture, health care, music, dance, 12literature, education, business, hospitality, journalism, sports, fashion, 13government, science, and technology; andSJR92,2,1714Whereas, Filipino American History Month has been celebrated in the United 15States since 1991, to commemorate the arrival of the first Filipinos in this country 16more than 425 years ago and to reflect on the notable contributions of Filipino 17Americans to the United States; andSJR92,2,2118Whereas, it is essential to promote the understanding, education, and 19appreciation of Filipino American history and culture, as the roles of Filipino 20Americans have largely been overlooked in the writing, teaching, and learning of 21the history of the United States;
