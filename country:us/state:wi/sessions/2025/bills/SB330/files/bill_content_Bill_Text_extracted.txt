Referred to Committee on Licensing, Regulatory Reform, State and Federal Affairs.SB330,1,21An Act to amend 175.35 (title), 175.35 (2) (d) and 175.35 (2g) (c) 4. c. of the 2statutes; relating to: waiting period for purchase of handguns.Analysis by the Legislative Reference BureauCurrent law provides that, before a federally licensed firearms dealer may transfer a handgun after a sale, the dealer must request that the Department of Justice perform a background check on the prospective purchaser to determine if the purchaser is prohibited from possessing a firearm.This bill prohibits the dealer from transferring the handgun to the purchaser until 48 hours have passed since the firearms dealer requested the background check on the purchaser.The people of the state of Wisconsin, represented in senate and assembly, do enact as follows:SB330,13Section 1. 175.35 (title) of the statutes is amended to read:SB330,1,44175.35 (title) Purchase Waiting period for the purchase of handguns.SB330,25Section 2. 175.35 (2) (d) of the statutes is amended to read:SB330,2,56175.35 (2) (d) The Forty-eight hours have elapsed from the time that the 1firearms dealer has received an approval a confirmation number regarding the 2firearms restrictions record search under sub.
